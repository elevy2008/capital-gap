About

Sophia Blackwelder

I’m interested in transactions involving institutions — whether they’re hospitals, law firms, financial institutions, software companies, or industrial businesses. I like understanding how organizations grow, merge, and create value.

I’ve always been interested in institutions and how they shape society. I started by studying healthcare systems through research, expanded into public policy and entrepreneurship through the NYC Wellness Alliance, and became fascinated by how business, law, and capital interact. Investment banking is where I can learn how organizations are built, financed, and transformed — before eventually pursuing a career advising companies on their most important strategic decisions.

The Capital Gap grew out of that interest: a research project asking why areas of enormous unmet need — a disease affecting 190 million people, a state that builds sophisticated markets for catastrophe risk but not for preventing it — don’t always attract capital in proportion to that need. Each issue is a case study in the same underlying question: how do investors decide what counts as an investable market, and what happens to the demand that falls outside that definition?
