A research project on capital allocation

Where the money in women’s health does and doesn’t go.

The Capital Gap is a series of short research briefs that examine women’s health not as an advocacy issue, but as a capital-allocation problem: which conditions attract investment, which don’t, and why the gap between disease burden and dollars persists even where the commercial case is strong.

  • ISSUE 01

    The Missing Middle: Why Capital Skips Women’s Chronic Health

    Only 6% of private healthcare capital reaches conditions affecting women — and nearly all of it clusters around cancers, reproductive health, and maternity. Endometriosis, as a case study, shows exactly where that capital stops flowing, and why.
