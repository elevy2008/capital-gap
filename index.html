A research project on capital allocation

Why doesn’t obvious demand always produce a functioning market?

The Capital Gap investigates a simple question: why do areas of enormous unmet need not always attract proportional investment? Through market research, case studies, and capital-flow analysis, each issue examines where capital enters a sector, what incentives shape its deployment, where financing mechanisms break down, and what prevents capital from reaching the people and assets that need it most — from women’s health markets to catastrophe-exposed real estate to American farmland.

Issues

  • ISSUE 01

    The Missing Middle: Why Capital Skips Women’s Chronic Health

    Only 6% of private healthcare capital reaches conditions affecting women — and nearly all of it clusters around cancers, reproductive health, and maternity. Endometriosis, as a case study, shows exactly where that capital stops flowing, and why.

    Published
  • ISSUE 02

    The Florida Resilience Capital Gap: Why Is It Easier to Finance Catastrophe Risk Than Prevent It?

    Florida has built one of the most sophisticated catastrophe-risk financing systems in the country — reinsurance, a state catastrophe fund, and billions in institutional capital-markets money. It hasn’t built an equally scalable system for financing the resilience that would reduce that risk before the next storm.

    Published
  • ISSUE 03

    The Farm Ownership Capital Gap: Why Is It Easier to Invest in Farmland Than to Become a Farmer?

    Farmland is one of institutional finance’s most reliably loved assets. 52% of the landlords who rent out U.S. farmland have never farmed it themselves — the clearest sign that capital has learned to price the land without learning to finance the farmer.

    Published
