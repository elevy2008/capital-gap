Methodology

How this is researched

Each issue of The Capital Gap asks a version of the same question: why does capital flow toward some health conditions and not others, given comparable disease burden? That question only holds up if the numbers behind it do.

Sourcing standard

Every statistic cited is traced to a named, publicly checkable source — a published report, a peer-reviewed study, or a named research institution — linked directly in the text or listed in a sources section at the end of the issue. Figures from advocacy organizations are used only when they cite an underlying primary dataset, and that primary source is linked instead where possible.

Primary sources used

  • Institutional research: World Economic Forum, McKinsey Health Institute, Boston Consulting Group
  • Government and public-health data: NIH, CDC, WHO
  • Market-sizing reports from healthcare-focused research firms, cross-checked across at least two independent estimates where they disagree
  • Peer-reviewed journal articles for prevalence, funding, and clinical-trial figures

What counts as a "gap"

A funding gap is only asserted where two things are both true: the condition carries a documented disease burden (prevalence, years lived with disability, economic cost), and there is a measurable shortfall in research funding, venture capital, or clinical-trial activity relative to that burden or to a comparable condition. Burden alone isn’t treated as proof of a market failure — the brief has to show where the capital specifically stops.

Limitations

Venture and private-market funding data for specific disease categories is incomplete and inconsistently reported across databases, so figures are presented as directional estimates from the best available public sources, not as precise totals. Where estimates vary meaningfully between sources, the range is shown rather than a single number.

Corrections

If you find a sourcing error or an outdated figure, I want to know about it. Email sophia.blackwelder@duke.edu and corrections will be reflected in the issue with a visible changelog note.
